About Me

Hi there! My name is Ishani. I'm a rising junior studying Computer Science with a minor in Machine Learning at Carnegie Mellon University. I live in Cupertino, CA but I'm originally from Pune, India.

At CMU, I have taken a wide variety of courses on topics such as Theoretical Computer Science, Data Structures and Algorithms, Computer Systems, Type Theory and Probability and Statistics. In addition, during my sophomore year (2019-2020), I served as a research assistant on Prof. Jan Hoffman's team. As a research assistant, I worked on Nomos, a programming language for smart contracts. I added functional constructs to the language, developed a theory for its subtyping and implemented this algorithm in the typechecker. I also served as an EXCEL Leader during the 2019-2020 school year. As an EXCEL Leader for classes on Discrete Math and 3D-Calc, I conducted weekly sessions of 6-10 students, prepared handouts for the sessions, and implemented various collaborative learning techniques in my sessions.

In the summer of 2019, I interned at the IBM TJ Watson Research Center in Yorktown Heights, NY on the Cloud Resource Management Team. As an intern, I contributed to the development of a Kubernetes Operator that handled the queueing, dispatching and resource management of Apache Spark jobs in a multi-cluster environment. I implemented features to make the operator more applicable to a wide range of use cases and to quickly detect job failures for redispatch. I also carried out performance testing for the operator. In the summer of 2020, I was a STEP Intern at Google.

In my free time, I like reading fantasy novels, cooking and playing games. I love listening to music, especially Hindi music. Recently, I got into Bollywood Dance and I'm working on getting better at it. My dad is a software engineer at SAP while my mom is a part-time teacher.
